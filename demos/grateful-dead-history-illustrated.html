Brand storytelling · The Dead

Thirty years. Twenty-three hundred shows.
One band that rewrote the playbook.

The Grateful Dead never had a #1 single. They sold out stadiums for three decades by trusting their audience and giving them more — not less.

1965 — 1971 · The acid years

Born out of
San Francisco

Formed in Palo Alto as The Warlocks, then renamed Grateful Dead in 1965. House band for Ken Kesey's Acid Tests — the first improvised, unscripted live shows in rock.

1972 — 1986 · The touring machine

Wall of Sound +
tape culture

Built the largest concert sound system ever assembled (1974) and openly invited fans to record every show. The "tape trader" Deadhead network turned the live circuit into the product itself.

1987 — 1995 · Mainstream + legacy

Touch of Grey
+ the final show

Their only Top 10 single hit in 1987, twenty-two years in. Highest-grossing touring act of the early 1990s. Jerry Garcia's last show: Soldier Field, July 9, 1995.
