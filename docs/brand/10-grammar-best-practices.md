# Grammar best practices

As an Enterprise-level, global company, we **must** maintain an impeccable level of English in our external communication with the world. Poor grammar makes us look sloppy, very local, and — simply put — unprofessional.

Here's a set of basic guidelines you can follow to ensure proper grammar.

⚠️ **Remember:** You have access to native speakers and content professionals who can help you at Didomi. In doubt, reach out to a native English speaker in your team, or send a message to Octavia Bermbach or Thierry Maout — they'll be happy to help.

## Grammar best practices and guidelines

### Always use American English

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| colour, honour, personalise/personalisation, customise/customisation, organise/organisation | color, honor, personalize/personalization, customize/customization, organize/organization |

### Didomi is always capitalized

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| The didomi team is here to support you. | The Didomi team is here to support you. |

### Use active voice

Try to use active voice as often as possible. This makes the content more engaging and breathable. In active voice, the subject of the sentence does the action.

| Don't (passive) | Do (active) |
|---|---|
| "The PMP was bought by Romain" | "Romain bought a PMP" |
| "The CMP was implemented by Orange" | "Orange implemented the CMP" |

### Always spell out acronyms the first time

While we're all using data privacy acronyms every day, not all our collaborators are necessarily as savvy.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "The CPRA will go live on (...)" | "The California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) will go live on (...)" |

### Product words are capitalized and never translated

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "La plateforme de gestion du consentement de didomi est une (...)" | "La Consent Management Platform (CMP) de Didomi est une (...)" |

### Use exclamation marks sparingly

Exclamation marks are great to show excitement, but they can also be quite awkward in a sentence. You should never use several in a row ("!!!"). Using one or two maximum per article/email/message is a good limit.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "Introducing our new Privacy Request Module!!!" | "Introducing our new Privacy Request Module." |

### Watch out for these words: Never plural

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| Performances, Informations, Consents, Vendors (when used in front of another noun, e.g. "vendors list") | Performance, Information, Consent, Vendor list, vendor tracking, vendor report |

### Apostrophes

Don't use an apostrophe when pluralizing an acronym, or with Didomi + a noun.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| API's can be used… | APIs can be used… |
| Didomi CSM's are highly trained. | Didomi CSMs are highly trained. |
| Didomi's team is here to help. | The Didomi team is here to help. |
| Didomi's platform is the leading consent management solution on the market. | The Didomi platform is the leading consent management solution on the market. |
