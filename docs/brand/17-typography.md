# Typography

## **Montserrat**

To make our messages clear and our voice loud, we chose a popular typography originally created by [Julieta Ulanovsky](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Montserrat/about?query=montserr) inspired by posters, signs and painted windows from the first half of the 20th century, seen in the Montserrat neighborhood of Buenos Aires.

**A strong and committed typography** that allows us, thanks to its multitude of available weights, to create rhythm, to structure our speech and our messages so that they are as clear as possible.

## **Authentic**

To highlight, stimulate, engage and speak from human to human, we accompany our main font, Montserrat, with the Authentic typography. This allows us to accentuate our messages, bringing the human being back to the center of the matter thanks to its handwritten aspect.

_The use of Authentic typography is not mandatory, but should be used sparingly and judiciously._
