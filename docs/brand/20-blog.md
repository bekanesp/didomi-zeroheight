# Blog

One of Didomi's strengths is the quality and variety of our blog content. Each post features a **hero visual** that introduces the topic at a glance and **body visuals** that illustrate key ideas and keep readers engaged throughout the article.

## **Blog post visuals are created to capture readers' interest and visually support the article's topic**

**Hero visuals:** The hero image is designed to immediately grab attention and convey the main idea or theme of the article through a compelling visual or illustration.

**Body visuals:** Images within the article are used to illustrate and reinforce key points, making the content more engaging and easier to understand.

---

### Hero Visual Guidelines

Our **hero visuals** follow a simple and efficient structure that makes it easy to create and publish new content regularly. Start from the dedicated visual template whenever possible.

- Frame size: Create blog hero visuals in a **1000 × 900 px** frame.
- Clip content: Make sure **Clip content** is enabled before exporting.
- Export format: Export the blog hero as an **AVIF file** using the ExportX plugin in Figma. This format keeps visual lightweight and high-quality, helping the website load quickly.

![](https://kleos.didomi.io/uploads/95bSrWYT1LRmo7IGzL6tAw.png)

#### Variation Examples

The design of **hero visuals** changes depending on the type of article. Case studies, country guides, and other formats each have their own look and feel.

A consistent **layout and color palette** keep everything on brand, while these small variations add rhythm and make browsing the blog feel more dynamic.

![](https://kleos.didomi.io/uploads/COjVCkAeN-UeO10YGssgdw.png)

---

### Body Visual Guidelines

- Frame size: Create blog content visuals in a **1740 px wide** frame. The height can vary to fit the content of the visual.
- Rounded corners: Corners should be **rounded to 30 px.**
- Clip content: Make sure **Clip content** is enabled before exporting.
- Export format: Export body visuals as **AVIF files** using the ExportX plugin in Figma.

![](https://kleos.didomi.io/uploads/pYfuQ6n2vtSAVtVydwraXA.png)

#### Variation Examples

The design of **blog body visuals** adapts to the story each article tells. Depending on the message, visuals can take many forms, from illustrations and quotes to maps, charts, or data tables. They not only help explain complex ideas but also add rhythm and visual breathing space, making each article more inviting to read.
