# Enterprise communication

What does it mean?

If we want to capture Enterprise customers, we need to talk like an Enterprise company, with experience, consistency, and strength.

Our **core Didomi identity** does not change, but is enhanced by:

- Increased professionalism
- Stronger image as a global player
- Clearer communication
- Value-driven messaging

We might be a tight-knit team and a scale-up, but as we increasingly move into the Enterprise sector, it's paramount that we live and breathe as an Enterprise company.

In practice, here are some guidelines to keep in mind and to understand how we should communicate as an Enterprise-first company.

---

## General enterprise communication and mindset guidelines

### "We" identity

When talking to customers, prospects or partners, always remember that you're representing the company and team as a whole. This shows that we are united, and speak as one.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "I've put together the attached presentation for you." | "We've prepared the attached presentation for you." or "Our team has prepared the attached presentation for you." |

---

### The competition

When talking about our competition, we take the high road and provide an educated stance about why Didomi makes sense for specific reasons and use cases.

We don't beg for people to do business with us. We know our worth and the value we can provide. We focus on our strengths first and never outright criticize our competition.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "OneTrust has bad UX, their product is impossible to understand." | "OneTrust offers a full suite of security-related products and tools, which is a great offering from companies that are looking for this sort of solutions. At Didomi, we focus on a more focused expertise around data privacy (...)" |

---

### Solutions over tool

As an Enterprise company, we solve problems and help our clients by providing both products and services.

We are not "just" selling products. The value of the solutions we provide is reflected in the quality of everything we do — and goes hand in hand with our pricing strategy.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "We sell a set of compliance tools." | "We help Enterprise companies address their compliance and data privacy challenges with tech solutions and services catered to their needs." |

---

### Global is natural to us

While we all know that our founding team and most of the early employees at Didomi were French, we are a global business with collaborators in dozens of countries, products available in over 40 languages, and English as our base language.

Being global should not be highlighted as something "special". This only has the opposite effect and makes us appear more local.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| Emphasize that we "even have employees in the US" or that we are "trying to expand to new markets outside of France/Europe". | Reassure customers in case the topic comes up that we have customers and collaborators all over the world. Be mindful of English grammar (see next section). |

---

### Collective expertise

When we talk about our products, our industry, or the future of data privacy, we are speaking with the collective intelligence of 150+ talented collaborators. Each of us has the support of experts in the legal, business, design, marketing, technology, and product fields.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| Feel like you have to do everything by yourself, and suggest that to external stakeholders. | Leverage our internal resources to find any answer or clarification you need. |

---

### A reassuring force

We have gone through the advent of GDPR and a lot more since. Every single Didomian can rely on a solid network, and so can our customers, prospect, and audience. When asked about "hot topics," we take a measured, objective approach.

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| Use overly-dramatic storytelling to leverage fear and over-the-top claims: "CPRA non-compliance will kill your business, we can help you" | Focus on our strengths, and provide reassurance that we can help our customers based on these strengths, using facts and data: "Our multi-regulations feature helps customers comply with regulations all over the world, including California. Our team of experts is here to help with the challenges you may be facing ahead of CPRA enforcement starting in July 2023." |
