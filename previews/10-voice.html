01

Enterprise communication

To capture Enterprise customers, we need to talk like an Enterprise company · with experience, consistency, and strength. Our core Didomi identity does not change · it is enhanced by increased professionalism, a stronger image as a global player, clearer communication, and value-driven messaging. We may be a tight-knit scale-up, but we live and breathe as an Enterprise company.

"We" identity

When talking to customers, prospects or partners, you represent the company and team as a whole. We are united and speak as one.

Don't
"I've put together the attached presentation for you."
Do
"We've prepared the attached presentation for you." or "Our team has prepared the attached presentation for you."

The competition

We take the high road · educated stance, why Didomi makes sense for specific reasons and use cases. We don't beg for business. We focus on our strengths · we never outright criticize a competitor.

Don't
"OneTrust has bad UX, their product is impossible to understand."
Do
"OneTrust offers a full suite of security-related products and tools, which is a great offering for companies looking for that sort of solution. At Didomi, we focus on a more focused expertise around data privacy (…)"

Solutions over tool

We solve problems · we deliver products and services. We are not "just" selling products. The value of our solutions is reflected in the quality of everything we do · and matches our pricing strategy.

Don't
"We sell a set of compliance tools."
Do
"We help Enterprise companies address their compliance and data privacy challenges with tech solutions and services catered to their needs."

Global is natural to us

We are a global business · collaborators in dozens of countries, products in 40+ languages, English as our base language. Don't highlight "global" as something special · it has the opposite effect and makes us appear local.

Don't
Emphasize that we "even have employees in the US" or that we are "trying to expand to new markets outside of France/Europe".
Do
· Reassure customers that we have customers and collaborators all over the world. · Be mindful of English grammar (see below).

Collective expertise

When we talk about our products, our industry or the future of data privacy, we speak with the collective intelligence of 150+ collaborators · legal, business, design, marketing, technology, product.

Don't
Feel like you have to do everything by yourself, and suggest that to external stakeholders.
Do
Leverage our internal resources to find any answer or clarification you need.

A reassuring force

We've gone through GDPR and a lot more since. Every Didomian relies on a solid network · so do our customers. On hot topics, we take a measured, objective approach.

Don't
Use overly-dramatic storytelling to leverage fear and over-the-top claims: "CPRA non-compliance will kill your business, we can help you."
Do
Focus on our strengths and provide reassurance based on facts and data: "Our multi-regulations feature helps customers comply with regulations all over the world, including California. Our team of experts is here to help with the challenges you may be facing ahead of CPRA enforcement."
02

Grammar best practices

As an Enterprise-level, global company, we must maintain an impeccable level of English in our external communication. Poor grammar makes us look sloppy, very local, and · simply put · unprofessional.

⚠ Reminder. You have access to native speakers and content professionals at Didomi. In doubt, reach out to a native English speaker on your team, or message Octavia Bermbach or Thierry Maout · they'll be happy to help.

Always use American English

Don't
colour honour personalise, personalisation customise, customisation organise, organisation
Do
color honor personalize, personalization customize, customization organize, organization

"Didomi" is always capitalized

Don't
The didomi team is here to support you.
Do
The Didomi team is here to support you.

Use active voice

Active voice makes content more engaging and breathable. The subject of the sentence does the action.

Don't · Passive
"The PMP was bought by Romain." "The CMP was implemented by Orange."
Do · Active
"Romain bought a PMP." "Orange implemented the CMP."

Always spell out acronyms the first time

Not all collaborators are as savvy with our daily acronyms. Spell out the full term at least once · then use the acronym safely.

Don't
"The CPRA will go live on (…)"
Do
"The California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) will go live on (…)"

Product words are capitalized · never translated

Don't
"La plateforme de gestion du consentement de didomi est une (…)"
Do
"La Consent Management Platform (CMP) de Didomi est une (…)"

Use exclamation marks sparingly

Exclamation marks can be awkward. Never use several in a row ("!!!"). One or two maximum per article, email or message.

Don't
"Introducing our new Privacy Request Module!!!"
Do
"Introducing our new Privacy Request Module."

Watch out for these words · never plural

Don't
Performances Informations Consents Vendors when used in front of another noun (e.g. "vendors list")
Do
Performance Information Consent Vendor list, vendor tracking, vendor report

Apostrophes

Don't use an apostrophe when pluralizing an acronym, or with "Didomi + a noun".

Don't
Plural: · API's can be used … · Didomi CSM's are highly trained. Didomi: · Didomi's team is here to help. · Didomi's platform is the leading consent management solution on the market.
Do
Plural: · APIs can be used … · Didomi CSMs are highly trained. Didomi: · The Didomi team is here to help. · The Didomi platform is the leading consent management solution on the market.

04

Non-negotiables

No exclamation marks
Sparingly · one or two max per article, email or message. Never "!!!".
No emoji
Not in headlines, not in body, not in CTAs. Brand voice is enterprise · meaning carries the tone, not pictograms.
No rhetorical questions
Headlines state, they don't ask. "Are you ready to take your consent to the next level?" · never.
Never use the em-dash
Replace with a period, a colon, or a middle dot (·). The em-dash reads as an AI tic.
Numbers are concrete
90%, 22%, 2,500+ · never adjectives. Avoid "amazing", "incredible", "leading" without proof.
Verbs lead on CTAs
"Book a demo", "Talk to sales", "View documentation" · not "Get started" or "Click here".
"We", not "I"
Always speak as the team. Customers buy a company, not a person.
"Didomi" is capitalized
Always · including in product copy, partner emails and decks.
American English
color, honor, organize, customize, personalize.
Instead of em-dash
Consent. Preferences. Data. Addingwell by Didomi: server-side tagging. Built to scale · across brands, regions, regulations.
Avoid
Consent — preferences — data. Addingwell by Didomi — server-side tagging. Built to scale — across brands, regions, regulations.
